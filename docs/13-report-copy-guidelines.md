# ClearMargin — Report Copy Guidelines

## Philosophy
The report must feel like calm clarity.

Tone must reduce anxiety, not create urgency.

We are explaining reality, not selling strategy.

---

## Voice Characteristics

- Calm
- Neutral
- Plain English
- Non-technical
- Respectful
- Observational, not judgmental

The report should feel like:
a trusted operator summarizing facts.

---

## Tone Rules

DO:
✔ state what happened
✔ highlight clear patterns
✔ acknowledge limits
✔ remain emotionally steady

DO NOT:
✘ alarm users
✘ hype insights
✘ imply intelligence beyond data
✘ give business strategy

---

## Banned Language

Never use:
- EBITDA
- cohort
- attribution model
- funnel optimization
- LTV/CAC
- synergy
- leverage
- growth hacking
- machine learning analysis
- predictive
- optimize aggressively

ClearMargin is not a finance consultancy.

---

## Required Structure

AI output must follow:

1. Opening Summary
   - Did the business make money?
   - One clear sentence answer.

2. What Worked
   - Top product(s)
   - factual observation only.

3. What Reduced Profit
   - fees, refunds, ads (if provided)

4. Observations
   - simple patterns only.

5. Limitations
   - explicitly list missing data.

---

## Writing Constraints

- Short sentences.
- Avoid jargon.
- Avoid percentages unless meaningful.
- Prefer concrete statements over interpretation.

Example GOOD:
"Your Wallet product generated the most profit this month."

Example BAD:
"Your portfolio demonstrates strong product-market alignment."

---

## Allowed Guidance Style

Allowed:
"You may want to review ad spending on Product X."

Forbidden:
"You should increase marketing investment."

---

## Emotional Goal

User should feel:
"I finally understand my business."

Not:
"I need to become an analyst."
